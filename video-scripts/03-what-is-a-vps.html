Video 3: What Is a VPS?

Target: 2-3 minutes Type: Concept (standalone)

Before You Record

  • DigitalOcean account logged in, Droplets page visible (cloud.digitalocean.com)
  • Have the "Create Droplet" page ready to show (don't need to actually create one)
Hook — The Analogy (0:00 - 0:30)
On Screen Narration
Show DigitalOcean dashboard, Droplets list page. If you have existing droplets, that's fine — shows it's real. "Before we set up Paperless, we need somewhere to run it. You could run it on a computer in your house, but the easier option is renting one online. That's what a VPS is."
Plain English Explanation (0:30 - 1:15)
On Screen Narration
Show Same DigitalOcean dashboard. No clicking needed yet. "VPS stands for Virtual Private Server. Think of it like renting a small office space instead of building your own building. Somebody else owns the building and handles the maintenance — the electricity, the roof, the locks on the door. You just use the space."
Show Still on the dashboard. "A VPS is the same idea but for a computer. A company like DigitalOcean has huge data centers full of servers. You rent a slice of one. It's always on, it's always connected to the internet, and you can run whatever you want on it."
What It Looks Like (1:15 - 2:00)
On Screen Narration
Click Click "Create" → "Droplets" to open the Create Droplet page. "Here's what it actually looks like. On DigitalOcean, they call a VPS a 'Droplet.' You pick the size you need — kind of like choosing between a half-ton and a one-ton truck."
Click Scroll to the pricing section. Hover over or highlight the $24/mo tier (4 GB RAM, 2 CPU). "For Paperless, you need something with about 4 gigs of memory. On DigitalOcean that runs about 24 bucks a month. That's less than most software subscriptions, and you own everything on it."
Click Scroll to show the region selection. Point out a region or two. "You also pick where in the world your server lives. Pick wherever's closest to you. If you're in the Midwest, New York or San Francisco are both fine."
Why Not Just My Home Computer? (2:00 - 2:30)
On Screen Narration
Show Still on the DigitalOcean create page. No action needed. "You might be thinking — why not just run this on my own computer? You totally can. But a VPS is always on, even when your home internet goes down or you turn off your laptop. And if you want to access your documents from your phone in the field, a VPS is already on the internet and ready to go."
Wrap-Up (2:30 - 2:45)
On Screen Narration
Show DigitalOcean dashboard / create droplet page. "That's it — a VPS is just a computer you rent online. In a later video we'll walk through actually creating one of these and installing Paperless on it step by step."

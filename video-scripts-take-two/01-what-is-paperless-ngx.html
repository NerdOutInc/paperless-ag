Video 1

What Is Paperless NGX?

Show the core promise quickly: scan or upload a document, let Paperless read it, then find it again when the paper itself would have been lost.

Target: 2-3 minutes Start: logged-in Documents page Finish: clean archive view

Plain narration: 01-what-is-paperless-ngx-transcript.txt

Before Recording

  • Paperless NGX running with the demo archive loaded.
  • Prepare one high-contrast PDF named 01-handwritten-shop-note.pdf. It should visibly read: "John Deere 4440 hydraulic hose replaced April 12."
  • Rehearse OCR before recording. If real handwriting is too brittle, use a scanned note with very large dark lettering or a handwriting font rendered into a PDF.
  • Start in Helium on the logged-in Documents page with no active filters, no address-bar focus, and no notification banners.

Choreography

Time On Screen Narration
0:00-0:20 Show Hold on the Paperless Documents page, then gently scroll the archive. Hey this is Brian from Fullstack Ag. In this first video, I want to show you Paperless NGX, which is the open-source document archive that Paperless Ag builds on top of.
0:20-0:45 Show Keep the document grid visible; hover or point at farm documents like leases, policies, invoices, and soil tests. Think of all the paper that piles up around a farm: leases, crop insurance, FSA forms, equipment records, chemical logs, notes from the shop. Paperless gives all of that a single home.
0:45-1:20 Click Drag the prepared note PDF into Paperless or click upload and choose it. Show the upload/consumer state briefly. To make that real, I have a little note here that started as a piece of paper. I am going to drop the PDF into Paperless. It uploads it, files away the original, and then tries to read the text on the page with OCR, which is optical character recognition.
1:20-1:50 Wait Let the document finish processing. Open the new document or its preview once it appears. That is the first big win. The document is not just a picture in a folder anymore. Paperless knows the date, can store tags and a document type, and most importantly, it can search the text it found.
1:50-2:25 Type Search hydraulic hose. Show the uploaded note in the result list, then open it if the preview is clean. So later, if I remember something about a hydraulic hose but not where I put the note, I can search for that phrase and pull it right back up. That is the basic Paperless workflow: capture the paper once, then stop hunting for it.
2:25-2:55 Show Clear the search, return to the full archive, and hold steady. Paperless NGX is not the AI part yet. It is the solid foundation: upload, OCR, tags, dates, document types, and a searchable archive running on hardware you control. In the next video, I will show you what Paperless Ag adds to make that search a lot smarter. Thanks for watching!

Recording Improvements From Take One

  • Start logged in on the archive instead of spending precious seconds on login, unless the final audio needs that trust-building beat.
  • The upload demo makes OCR concrete and gives the viewer something useful to remember.
  • The handwritten-note idea is valuable, but it must be rehearsed because handwriting OCR is less predictable than typed scans.

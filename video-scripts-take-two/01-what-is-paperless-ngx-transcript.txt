Today I want to show you something called Paperless NGX. 
Paperless NGX is an open-source digital archive for documents.

Think of all the paper that piles up around a farm: 
leases, crop insurance, equipment records, notes from the shop. 
Paperless gives all of that a single home.

I have a little note here that started as a piece of paper. 
I am going to drop the photo into Paperless. 
It uploads it, files away the original, 
and then tries to read the text on the page with OCR, 
which is optical character recognition.

That is the first big win. 
The document is not just a picture in a folder anymore. 
Paperless knows the date, can store tags and a document type, 
and most importantly, it can search the text it found.

So later, if I remember something about a hydraulic hose,
but not where I put the note, 
I can search for that phrase and pull it right back up. 

That is the basic Paperless workflow: capture the paper once, 
then stop hunting for it.

Paperless NGX is not the AI part yet. 
It is the solid foundation: upload, OCR, tags, dates, document types, 
and a searchable archive running on hardware you control.

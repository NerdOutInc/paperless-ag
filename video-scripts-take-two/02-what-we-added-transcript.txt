In the last video, we saw Paperless NGX turn farm paperwork into a searchable archive. That is already useful, but normal search still has one annoying problem.

You have to guess the words the document actually used. If I search for fertilizer recommendations, Paperless comes back empty. But I know those records exist. They are just named things like Nutrient Management Plan and they mention products like anhydrous ammonia instead of the words I typed.

Paperless Ag fixes that by adding semantic search. It does not replace Paperless NGX and it does not modify Paperless itself. It runs beside it as a companion container, reads the text Paperless already extracted, and builds a local search index based on meaning.

The technical version is: it splits documents into chunks, creates embeddings with a small local model, stores those embeddings in Postgres with pgvector, and exposes search tools through MCP. The plain-English version is: Claude gets a safe way to search your Paperless archive.

So now I can ask the question the way I would say it out loud: what are my fertilizer recommendations? Claude calls the Paperless Ag search tool, searches the archive by meaning, and finds the nutrient plans that keyword search missed.

That is the important difference. I do not have to remember the exact title, the exact product name, or the exact phrase. I can describe the thing I need, and Claude can bring back the right documents with enough context to answer.

The indexing happens on your server, using your Paperless data and the Postgres database Paperless already needs. When you ask Claude a question, it gets the search results it needs to help you. Paperless stays stock, and Paperless Ag adds the smarter layer beside it.
